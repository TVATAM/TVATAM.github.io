Little Free Library





“ A library is the delivery room for the birth of ideas, a place where history comes to life. ”
– Norman Cousins


The mission of the founders of the Little Free Library is: to promote literacy and the love of reading by building free book exchanges worldwide and to build a sense of community as we share skills, creativity and wisdom across generations. There are over 32,000 Little Free Library book exchanges around the world, bringing curbside literacy home and sharing over 1 million books annually.




The idea was first introduced in Hudson, Wisconsin, in 2009 by Todd Bol. He designed a wooden container to look like a school house on a post on his lawn as a tribute to his mother, Esther Bol, who was a book lover and a school teacher.

The partner of Todd Bol, Rick Brooks, spread the word about the library and the idea spread at a rapid pace. Library owners can create their own library box. They are usually about the size of a doll house or they can purchase one from the website. For a fee, the libraries can be registered, if they are registered they get assigned a number at the organization's website. Owners receive a sign that reads "Little Free Library".

The original goal for this project, was to create 2,150 Little Libraries, which would surpass the number of libraries founded by Andrew Carnegie. As of January 2014, there are over 15,000 Little Libraries worldwide. An estimated 1,650,000 books were donated and borrowed from 2010 to 2013.

Some Free Little Libraries have been donated to areas that have no libraries of their own or have been affected by disasters. The Libraries are often created with materials found in the community were its roots lie.

There are libraries of all shapes and sizes. So keep your eyes open and maybe you find one.
